FORTKOCHI

A charming seaside area, Fort Kochi is known for its Dutch, Portuguese, and British colonial architecture, and elaborate bamboo fishing nets at Fort Kochi Beach. St. Francis Church was the original burial site of explorer Vasco da Gama. Upscale eateries and chic cafes serve Keralan specialties, and quaint shops sell cotton clothing and handmade souvenirs. Heritage buildings house contemporary art galleries.

KADAMAKKUDI

Kadamakudy Islands is an island suburb of the city of Kochi in the Indian state of Kerala. It is situated around 8 km north of the city centre Kadamakudy Islands is a cluster of fourteen islands: Valiya Kadamakudy (the main island), Murikkal, Palyam Thuruth, Pizhala, Cheriya Kadamakudy, Pulikkapuram, Moolampilly, Puthussery, Chariyam Thuruth, Chennur, Kothad, Korambadam, Kandanad and Karikkad Thuruthu.

VYPIN

Known for its long sand beaches, laid-back Vypin Island is a common stop on local ferry tours. Popular Cherai Beach is renowned for dolphin sightings, while quieter Kuzhuppilly Beach is flanked by coconut groves and offers safe swimming. The 1970s Vypin Lighthouse at Puthuvype Beach has an elevator, with sea views from the top. Casual eateries around Pallippuram Road serve homestyle Keralan cuisine.

JEW STREET

Jew Town is the narrow street between Mattancherry Palace and the Pardesi Synagogue. It is famous for the antique shops all along its sides. The colonial buildings lining the street add to its old-world charm.
